Our story / Roadside hospitality

Hospitality is how the road gets better.

Rangeway is a hospitality company that happens to charge vehicles.

The point is not to interrupt the journey. It is to make the journey feel cared for.

Origin / Operating point of view

Drivers are guests.

Stops are experiences.

The wait is the product.

Rangeway was founded from an unusual combination: years spent operating luxury resorts, wellness retreats, and flagship urban hotels, alongside firsthand experience in the EV-driver community.

The charging industry has largely treated the stop as a utility problem. We see a hospitality problem: light, shelter, clean facilities, calm service, and a place that respects the time between arrival and departure.

Every Rangeway format is designed as a place a traveler would choose, not merely tolerate. The scale changes with the road. The care does not.

Operating beliefs

Four decisions behind every stop.

01

Make the wait good.

If a driver has to wait, the stop has a responsibility to make that time useful and comfortable.

02

Operate the details.

Lighting, lavatories, provisions, seating, and service are operating decisions, not decorative features.

03

Plan real corridors.

We build around routes people actually drive and the communities those roads connect.

04

Hold one standard.

Waystation, Basecamp, and Summit should feel made by the same company and held to the same promise.

The story is being built in public.

Rangeway is developing projects, building partnerships, publishing Field Notes, raising capital, and hiring. The first locations remain in development.
